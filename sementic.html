INtro to coding

In today’s digital world, coding is no longer just a skill for computer scientists — it’s becoming a fundamental literacy for all students. Learning to code helps children understand how
the technology around them works and encourages them to become creators, not just consumers.
One of the biggest benefits of coding is that it teaches problem-solving. When students write code,
they learn how to break complex tasks into smaller, manageable steps. This logical thinking applies not just to tech, but to
real-life challenges as well. Coding also promotes creativity. Whether students are designing a simple game or building a website,
they’re using imagination and innovation to bring ideas to life. It boosts confidence as they see their code turn into something meaningful.
